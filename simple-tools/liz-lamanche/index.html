Artist Spotlight: Liz LaManche​

Liz LaManche is a Somerville-based public artist recognized for her vibrant, large-scale street murals across Boston. With training in architecture and software design, she creates bold public art that makes urban spaces more welcoming. LaManche's colorful interventions are designed to foster connection and shared experiences among city residents and visitors. Over a ten-year period, she has completed three Public Space Invitational projects that demonstrate how simple artistic gestures can fundamentally change how people interact with their surroundings.

Stairs of Fabulousness​

In 2014, Liz LaManche, a Boston-based visual artist, transformed the lobby of Boston City Hall. Using non-skid skateboard tape, Liz introduced a cascade of bright color on the edge of the stairs in City Hall's central mezzanine, an area with drab red bricks and austere concrete.

Liz responded to a surprisingly unused area of City Hall and was inspired by the stairs' frayed caution tape. Over a two-day installation, Liz installed 2,346 feet of non-skid tape on both the top and riser of each stair.

The mezzanine of City Hall is now the central meeting area in the building. Stairs of Fabulousness was a small intervention that made the space more welcoming. With the addition of tables and chairs, City employees and visitors to City Hall started to spend much more time on the mezzanine for lunch, meetings, and events. It helped kickstart many more changes to the mezzanine over the next few years, including the addition of a locally-owned coffee kiosk, Wi-Fi, and modern lighting.

Mystery Map​

Another simple, transformational tool: paint. Four years later, Liz transformed a sidewalk in East Boston. Shown here, Mystery Map is made of four sets of colored lines punctuated by circles, encouraging more than one participant at a time to puzzle out the best ways to follow the map or create their own games using it as a board. Liz LaManche, who has created several pieces of public art pieces throughout East Boston and the greater Boston area, emphasized simplicity to allow people of any age to enjoy and interact with the map in their own way.

TIPS​

Stairs of Fabulousness was one of the first projects we implemented through Public Space Invitational. For such a seemingly simple intervention, we did not realize that the property owner, in this case the City of Boston, would require the artist to get general liability insurance for the duration of the installation. Make sure to understand all of the permitting requirements, such as insurance and bonding, at the beginning of your planning process. And if you’re funding a project, make sure the artist has enough budget to cover these additional costs.
