Garden Delivery​

ArtistsChanel Thervil, Dorchester Art Project, JennyMae Kho, Brianna Asbury, Liz LaManche, Julie Ann Otis, Tran Vu
PartnerThe Trustees
LocationEl Jardín de la Amistad, Titus Sparrow Park, Nightingale Community Garden
DisciplineArt, Community, Green Spaces

At the beginning of the pandemic, people found themselves stuck at home, disconnected from community and nature. Many community gardens closed to the public, cutting off an important source of greenery and social connection. In response, we shifted our approach from public installations and events to bringing art and a piece of community gardens directly to people's homes.

Our goal was to distribute a collection of window boxes, pots, and birdhouses to 100 families and residents at three gardens in the South End, Roxbury, and Dorchester. Six teams of artists customized these containers over six weeks, each leading remote community engagement efforts to reconnect neighborhood residents to community gardens during the period of social distancing.

Artists Jenny Mae Kho and Brianna Asbury created seventeen custom painted and decoupaged planters for residents of Roxbury, featuring quotes from local leaders like Melnea Cass, Ella Little Collins, and Elma Lewis. Liz LaManche designed planters and birdhouses for the South End. She drew from the neighborhood's history as a place where communities supported one another, from the thriving Black culture that brought the Jazz Age to the gay and artist communities that found a haven there.

Chanel Thervil found inspiration by interviewing gardeners at El Jardín de la Amistad1. One gardener spoke about "the juxtaposition of intensive labor and meditative focus" in gardening. The contrast between these ideas led Chanel to work with opposing colors, choosing warm red and pale blue as base colors for the planters. Another gardener spoke about "the curve of life," which led Chanel to design each planter with abstract curvilinear forms.

Once the artwork was complete, we worked with The Trustees to plant a mix of tomatoes, peppers, and herbs in the pots and window boxes at City Natives in Mattapan, The Trustees' native plant nursery. We then connected with residents to distribute the items at the three gardens: El Jardín de la Amistad in Roxbury, Nightingale Community Garden in Dorchester, and Titus Sparrow Park in the South End.
