Trailer Made + Wheel'n Around​

Since 2018, we have worked with The Trustees and TD Bank to expand the reach of community gardens in Boston. Trailer Made is one of our favorite examples of a pop-up space that instantly activated underused spaces in community gardens. The mobile installation engaged hundreds of residents at three community gardens in Boston.

The landscape architects behind Trailer Made transformed a farm trailer from Texas and created a community learning and gathering space. They made the farm trailer beautiful with a new wooden structure, bright colors, flowers, a large community chalkboard, and repurposed stained glass. The project was also quite flexible — towable between spaces and outfitted with solar panels to provide electricity for lighting and events.

The following year, we worked with the team to bring Wheel'n Around to the Fenway Victory Gardens. The project was a mobile installation that transformed a large set of colorful wheelbarrows into social gathering spaces. The wheelbarrows were used for seating, board games, planters, informational signage, and tables. The installation proved to be just as versatile as Trailer Made, moving between community gardens and making an appearance in a temporary parklet on PARK(ing) Day1.

Footnotes​

  1. Originating in San Francisco in 2005, PARK(ing) Day is an annual event in cities around the world where people turn parking spaces next to sidewalks into enjoyable public spaces. ↩
